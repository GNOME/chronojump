Construction of Chronopic_juanfer prototype

Juan Fernando Pardo has started to release an own Chronopic version, simpler and cheaper.

He managed to build it in one hour, and the total price in Argentina is like 22 US$

If you want to use the other Chronopic -more expensive (70 Euros in Spain)- check the construction page.

Construction of the Chronopic_juanfer v.1.1 (February, 9, 2007)

Chronopic_juanfer schema. Notes:
  • All the components are mounted on a prototyping board
  • Red, Brown and Orange lines are wires located at the bottom face of the board. Both extremes of the wire are soldered to the pins of the components.
  • The blue line is wire located at the top face of the board. Both extremes of the wire are soldered to the pins of the componentes.
  • It is recommend the use of sockets for the MAX232 and PIC16F876A components, instead of soldering them directly to the board.
Bill of materials
  • C1: 100nF capacitor
  • C2: 1uF 63v Electrolytic Capacitor
  • C3: 1uF 50v Electrolytic Capacitor
  • C4,C5,C6,C7: 22uF/25v Electrolytic Capacitors
  • C8,C9: 22pF ceramic capacitors
  • X1: 4Mhz quartz crystal oscillator
  • CL1,CL2: two terminals package for printed circuit boards
  • R1: 10K resistor 5%, 1/4W
  • R2: 147 ohms resistor. 5%. 1/4W
  • R3: 330 ohms resistor. 5%. 1/4W
  • S1,S2: Tack switches for printed circuit boards
  • L1: 3mm green Led diode
  • MAX232: The max232 chip. RS232-TTL conversor (mounted in a 2x18 pin socket)
  • PIC16F876A: Michochip Pic 16F876 microcontroller. 28 DIL package. (mounted in a 2x14 pin socket).
  • -One 2x8 socket for the Max232 chip
  • -One 2x14 socket for the PIC16f876a chip
  • -A 8x5 cm prototyping board
